Special NH Considerations

üè† Old NH Homes

Many NH homes are historic with single-pane windows and uneven heating. Use draft stoppers, monitor temperature swings, and consider window insulation film in winter. Radiator heat can be extremely drying - keep plants at safe distances.

üå°Ô∏è Temperature Swings

NH can have 40¬∞F temperature differences in a day! Keep plants away from doors that open frequently. Consider using a min/max thermometer to track temperature ranges in your plant areas.

üí° Short Winter Days

From November through February, NH gets less than 10 hours of daylight. Full-spectrum LED grow lights on timers (12-14 hours) can make a huge difference for light-loving plants.

üíß Water Quality

NH water varies by town - well water may be hard, city water may have chlorine/chloramine. Let tap water sit overnight or use filtered water for sensitive plants like carnivorous plants or orchids.

üå™Ô∏è Mud Season

March-April brings high humidity and temperature fluctuations. Watch for fungal issues and ensure good air circulation. This is prime time for fungus gnats - use sticky traps preventatively.
