Watering Wisdom

Check soil moisture with your finger. Most houseplants prefer slightly dry soil between waterings. In NH winters, reduce watering frequency as plants grow slower.

Light Requirements

NH winters are dark! Move plants closer to south-facing windows or consider grow lights. Rotate plants weekly for even growth.

Humidity Hacks

NH indoor air gets dry in winter. Group plants together, use pebble trays with water, or run a humidifier to keep tropical plants happy.

Fertilizing Schedule

Feed monthly in spring/summer, reduce to every 6-8 weeks in fall/winter. Use half-strength liquid fertilizer for most houseplants.

Pruning & Propagation

Spring is perfect for pruning in NH! Save healthy cuttings to propagate in water. Share with friends or expand your collection!

Pest Prevention

Check new plants for 2 weeks before introducing to your collection. Wipe leaves monthly with neem oil solution to prevent pests.

Repotting Guide

Repot when roots circle the bottom or emerge from drainage holes. Spring is ideal. Choose a pot 1-2 inches larger in diameter.

Leaf Care

Dust leaves monthly for better photosynthesis. Use a damp cloth for smooth leaves, soft brush for fuzzy ones. Shower plants quarterly!

Common Mistakes

Overwatering kills more plants than underwatering. Yellow leaves often mean too much water. Brown tips suggest low humidity or fluoride in tap water.

üö® Plant Emergency Guide

Yellowing Leaves? Usually overwatering. Check soil, improve drainage, reduce watering frequency.

Brown Leaf Tips? Low humidity or chemicals in water. Use filtered water and increase humidity.

Drooping Plant? Check soil moisture first. Could be too dry OR too wet. Assess light and temperature too.

Leggy Growth? Not enough light. Move closer to window or add grow lights. Prune to encourage bushiness.

No New Growth? Could be dormant (winter), needs fertilizer, or pot-bound. Check roots and feeding schedule.

Pests Found? Isolate immediately! Spray with neem oil or insecticidal soap. Repeat weekly until gone.
